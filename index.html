The problem

Eating out is the new normal — and it's where good intentions fall apart.

Americans now eat a third of their calories away from home — and every existing app reacts after the plate is empty. Forkcast acts before you order.

Guesswork, not data

Independent restaurants rarely publish protein, sodium, or carbs — federal labeling rules only cover chains.

Too late to help

Log-after apps are accounting, not coaching — and menu labels alone shift intake by only ~24 calories per order.

How it works

Your health profile becomes a daily eating-out plan.

Four steps. No calorie diary required to get value on day one.

01

Build your profile

Height, weight, age, activity, and goal — we compute your targets with clinical formulas (Mifflin-St Jeor), plus allergies and conditions to watch.

02

See what fits

Every nearby dish gets a personal Fit Score from calories, protein, fiber, sodium, and sugar — ranked against what's left of your day.

03

Order or dine in

Build a basket and send it to the restaurant, or just walk in — either way, your plan updates the moment you commit.

04

Confirm the meal

Confirmed orders pre-fill your log; photos cover everything else. Every entry keeps its source, portion, and confidence.
