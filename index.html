The problem

Eating out is the new normal — and it's where good intentions fall apart.

Americans now eat about a third of their calories away from home. Those meals run ~200 calories and ~350mg more sodium than home cooking — and we're terrible at guessing how much. Even mandatory calorie labels move intake by only about 24 calories per order.

Today's apps make you log after you've already eaten. That's accounting, not coaching. Forkcast flips it: decide what fits before you order.

Guesswork, not data

Menus rarely show protein, carbs or sodium for independent restaurants.

Too late to help

Photo-calorie apps tell you the damage once the plate is empty.

How it works

Your health profile becomes a daily eating-out plan.

Four steps. No calorie diary required to get value on day one.

01

Build your cabinet

Enter height, weight, age, activity and goal. We compute your BMI, BMR/TDEE and macro targets with real formulas.

02

Get matched dishes

Every nearby dish gets a personal Fit Score — calories, protein density, fiber, sodium, sugar — ranked for you.

03

Order or walk in

Add it to your day in one tap. Delivery, pickup, or just go eat — your plan updates instantly.

04

Snap to confirm

Photo-log what you actually ate. We reconcile it against your plan and your weekly trend.
