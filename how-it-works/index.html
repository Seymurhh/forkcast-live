How it works

Real formulas. Honest estimates. A recommendation you can act on.

Forkcast turns your body metrics into daily targets, scores every nearby dish against them, and closes the loop with a quick photo. No black boxes — here's exactly how each part works.

01

Your health cabinet

Enter height, weight, age, sex, activity and goal. We compute BMI, then BMR via the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, multiply by an activity factor for TDEE, and apply a safe deficit or surplus. Macros follow ISSN protein guidance (up to 2.0 g/kg when cutting), ~27% fat, the rest carbs, with a 14g-fiber-per-1000-kcal target.

02

The Fit Score

Every dish gets a personal 0–100 score: calorie appropriateness for one meal, protein density, fiber, sodium and sugar — weighted by your goal. It's explainable: you see exactly why (High protein · Good fiber · Watch sodium), never a mystery number.

03

Decide before you order

Nearby restaurants and dishes reorder around your day. Add a dish in one tap — delivery, pickup, or just walk in. Your remaining calories and macros update instantly, so the next decision is informed too.

04

Snap to close the loop

Photograph what you actually ate. Our vision model estimates calories and macros, you confirm or correct, and it reconciles against your plan and weekly trend. Logging is a feature here — not the whole job.

Inside the Fit Score

Five sub-scores, weighted by your goal.

Each is normalized 0–1, then combined and scaled to 100. Weights shift for weight-loss vs. muscle-building goals — e.g. losing weight leans harder on calorie fit and protein.

Calorie fit

30–34%

How well calories match a single meal's share of your daily target. Overshooting is penalized harder than undershooting.

Protein density

30–36%

Share of calories from protein. ≥30% earns full marks — the lever for satiety and lean mass.

Fiber

12%

Rewards 8g+ per meal — satiety and metabolic health.

Sodium

12–16%

Full marks ≤600mg, zero by 2000mg. Restaurant food's biggest hidden cost.

Sugar

10–14%

Full marks ≤8g, penalized toward 35g. Catches 'healthy' bowls that aren't.

How it fits together

Two sides, one nutrition layer.

Diners get personalized recommendations; restaurants get discovered by health-minded customers. In between sits the part we obsess over — turning any menu, from chains to independents, into trustworthy per-dish nutrition.

A

You

Your profile becomes daily targets, every nearby dish gets a Fit Score, and a quick photo keeps your day honest.

B

The nutrition layer

We turn any restaurant's menu into per-dish calories and macros — the part no off-the-shelf database covers — and always show how confident we are.

C

Restaurants

Partners upload a menu, get instant nutrition, and surface their best dishes to people who came to eat well.

Honesty by design: photo-based calorie estimation has a physics-bound error floor (~16–25%). Forkcast leads with menu name + description (where models match nutritionists), grounds estimates in trusted nutrition data, and shows ranges — we sell "fast, good-enough, easy to correct," never clinical accuracy.

The detailed system architecture and data pipeline are shared with partners and investors on request.
